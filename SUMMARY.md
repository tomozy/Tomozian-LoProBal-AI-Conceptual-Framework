### CONCEPTUAL FRAMEWORK THEORY
LoProBal, an acronym for Logic, Probability, and Balance, is a structural heuristic and foundational lexicon developed by industrial designer Eskil Tomozy in March 2026. It functions as a dynamic, programmatic system designed to evaluate meaning, data, and conditions, starting from an absolute absence of data and building toward complete, mutual interactions. Central to the system is its calculation of balance, which dynamically weighs logic and probability against states of symmetry or asymmetry, continuously adjusting itself to maintain focus and execute precise actions. Crucially, this mathematical engine is governed by sovereign ethical laws requiring the strict absence of harm, a mandate to serve, and absolute user autonomy. Any violation instantly triggers a failsafe (eject) that halts the system, ensuring its continuous evolution remains permanently tethered to user safety. Start tight (Logic), determine how loose (Probability), then adjust (Balance).

### CONCEPTUAL STRUCTURAL METAPHOR
The design establishes the site datum (0.0) and installs a primary structural core (1.0) as the principal redundancy against failure. When an external dynamic load (2.1) impacts the cladding, that kinetic force is transferred onto the primary framing (2.2).The assembly is monitored at critical joints (2.3), where the balance **Δ** is tracked via integrated strain sensors. Once non-uniform stress is identified, the geometry facilitates a load redistribution (2.4) to divert the axial force through a redundant truss, directing it into a stabilized triangulated support. This equilibrated state is maintained to support the total gravity loads (2.5), before the mechanical stress data is integrated into the Digital Twin (3.0) for continuous health monitoring.


### CONCEPTUAL NEURAL METAPHOR
Within the foundational cortex (0.0), the Prefrontal Cortex (1.0) establishes top-down regulation as the primary logic gate. This balanced state is executed through the Brainstem (2.5), before the interaction is integrated into long-term memory via the Hippocampus (3.0).
